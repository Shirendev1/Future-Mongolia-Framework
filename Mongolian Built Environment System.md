# Mongolia Built Environment System

## Vision
Develop a human-centered and climate-adaptive construction standard for Mongolia.

## Key Principles

1. Human Scale Cities
Cities should prioritize walkability and community spaces rather than high-rise density.

2. Climate Adaptive Architecture
Buildings must respond to Mongolia's extreme climate conditions.

3. Circular Construction
Promote reuse of materials and low-carbon construction methods.

4. Regional Design
Architecture should reflect Mongolia’s different ecological regions.

5. Smart Infrastructure
Integrate energy monitoring, water recycling, and digital infrastructure.

## System Components

1. Urban Planning Model  
Human-scale city design with mixed-use neighborhoods.

2. Construction Standards  
Energy efficiency, insulation, and structural resilience adapted to Mongolia's climate.

3. Green Infrastructure  
Integration of green corridors, urban forests, and water systems.

4. Regional Architecture  
Design guidelines adapted to Mongolia’s different ecological regions.

5. Digital Infrastructure  
Smart monitoring for energy, water, and environmental impact.

## Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1 – Research and Standards Development  
Define construction guidelines and sustainability metrics.

Phase 2 – Pilot Projects  
Test sustainable building models in selected regions.

Phase 3 – National Policy Integration  
Align standards with Mongolia’s national urban development policy.

Phase 4 – International Collaboration  
Cooperate with EU institutions and sustainability research groups.

## Ger District Transformation System

Transform Mongolia’s traditional ger districts into modern eco-districts while preserving community identity and improving living conditions.

## Sustainable Housing Communities

Develop low-density residential neighborhoods based on Mongolia’s cultural lifestyle and environmental conditions.

### Key Principles

1. *House-Based Communities*  
Encourage development of eco-friendly housing communities instead of high-rise residential buildings.

2. *Human-Scale Neighborhoods*  
Design walkable streets with parks, playgrounds, and shared community spaces.

3. *Energy Efficient Homes*  
Use high-quality insulation, renewable energy systems, and climate-adaptive building techniques suitable for Mongolia’s cold climate.

4. *Green Living Environment*  
Integrate trees, green corridors, gardens, and small parks within residential areas.

5. *Local Services Access*  
Ensure each community includes essential services such as schools, childcare centers, small shops, and healthcare facilities within walking distance.

- Эрчим хүчний хэмнэлттэй дулаан барилгууд  
- Мод, ногоон бүс, цэцэрлэгтэй орчин  
- Сургууль, цэцэрлэг, үйлчилгээ ойрхон байх


### Key Principles

1. *Infrastructure First*  
Develop essential infrastructure including clean water supply, sewage systems, district heating, electricity networks, and road access.

2. *Gradual Housing Upgrade*  
Support residents to gradually upgrade from traditional housing to insulated eco-houses without forced relocation.

3. *Green Neighborhood Design*  
Introduce green streets, tree corridors, community parks, and pedestrian-friendly public spaces.

4. *Renewable Energy Integration*  
Encourage solar panels, energy-efficient heating systems, and other sustainable energy solutions.

5. *Community-Centered Development*  
Ensure local participation in planning and development to maintain strong neighborhood communities.
