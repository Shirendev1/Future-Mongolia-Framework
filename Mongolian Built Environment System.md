# Mongolia Built Environment System

## Vision
Develop a human-centered and climate-adaptive construction standard for Mongolia.

## Key Principles

1. Human Scale Cities
Cities should prioritize walkability and community spaces rather than high-rise density.

2. Climate Adaptive Architecture
Buildings must respond to Mongolia's extreme climate conditions.

3. Circular Construction
Promote reuse of materials and low-carbon construction methods.

4. Regional Design
Architecture should reflect Mongolia’s different ecological regions.

5. Smart Infrastructure
Integrate energy monitoring, water recycling, and digital infrastructure.

## System Components

1. Urban Planning Model  
Human-scale city design with mixed-use neighborhoods.

2. Construction Standards  
Energy efficiency, insulation, and structural resilience adapted to Mongolia's climate.

3. Green Infrastructure  
Integration of green corridors, urban forests, and water systems.

4. Regional Architecture  
Design guidelines adapted to Mongolia’s different ecological regions.

5. Digital Infrastructure  
Smart monitoring for energy, water, and environmental impact.

## Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1 – Research and Standards Development  
Define construction guidelines and sustainability metrics.

Phase 2 – Pilot Projects  
Test sustainable building models in selected regions.

Phase 3 – National Policy Integration  
Align standards with Mongolia’s national urban development policy.

Phase 4 – International Collaboration  
Cooperate with EU institutions and sustainability research groups.
