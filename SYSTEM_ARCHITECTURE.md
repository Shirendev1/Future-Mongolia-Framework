# SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

This document describes the five key layers of the system architecture:

## 1. Governance Layer
The Governance Layer is responsible for establishing the frameworks, regulations, and policies that guide decision-making and resource allocation within the system. It ensures accountability, transparency, and stakeholder engagement in the management of resources and projects.

### Interactions:
- Collaborates with all layers to align strategies and policies.
- Engages stakeholders to gather input and feedback on governance practices.

## 2. Digital & Knowledge Infrastructure
This layer includes the technological tools, platforms, and systems that facilitate data collection, analysis, and dissemination. It ensures that stakeholders have access to the information needed for informed decision-making.

### Interactions:
- Works with the Governance Layer to ensure compliance with regulations and policies.
- Provides data and insights to the Sustainable Industry layer to optimize operations and reduce environmental impact.

## 3. Sustainable Industry
The Sustainable Industry layer focuses on creating economic activities that minimize environmental impact and promote social equity. This involves industry practices that are resource-efficient, reduce waste, and promote sustainable development.

### Interactions:
- Partner with the Digital & Knowledge Infrastructure for data-driven insights.
- Coordinates with the Circular Economy to optimize resource use and recycling.

## 4. Circular Economy
This layer promotes the design of products and services that minimize waste and encourage the continual use of resources. It aims to create closed-loop systems where waste is reused, recycled, or repurposed.

### Interactions:
- Collaborates with the Sustainable Industry to implement practices that reduce waste.
- Works closely with the Digital & Knowledge Infrastructure to track resource flows and manage materials efficiently.

## 5. International Collaboration
The International Collaboration layer encourages partnerships and cooperation across borders to share knowledge, technology, and best practices. This collaboration is essential for addressing global challenges such as climate change and resource depletion.

### Interactions:
- Engages with all layers to foster synergies and joint initiatives.
- Partners with the Governance Layer to ensure that international policies align with local regulations and practices.
