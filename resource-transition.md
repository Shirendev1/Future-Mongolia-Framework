# Resource Transition Strategy for Mongolia

## Overview

Mongolia’s economy has long depended on the extraction and export of natural resources, particularly mining. While this sector has generated significant revenue, it has also created economic vulnerability, environmental pressure, and limited diversification.

This strategy outlines a transition toward a more balanced and sustainable economic structure.

## Key Goals

- Reduce dependence on raw resource extraction
- Diversify the national economy
- Promote value-added industries
- Support sustainable development
- Strengthen long-term economic resilience

## Strategic Directions

### 1. Industrial Diversification
Encourage the development of manufacturing industries that process domestic resources into higher-value products.

### 2. Green Energy Development
Expand renewable energy infrastructure including solar and wind power.

### 3. Circular Resource Use
Promote reuse and recycling of materials to reduce demand for new resource extraction.

### 4. Technology and Innovation
Invest in digital technologies, research institutions, and innovation ecosystems.

### 5. Human Capital Development
Strengthen education and skills training to support a knowledge-based economy.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal is to transform Mongolia into a diversified economy where mining is only one component of a broader, sustainable development system.
